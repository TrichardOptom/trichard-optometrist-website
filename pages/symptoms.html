Common Eye Symptoms

Your Eyes Tell an Important Story

Many eye conditions begin with small symptoms that should never be ignored. Click on any symptom below to learn more and discover when it may be time to book an appointment.

Common Eye Symptoms

Click on any symptom below to learn more.

Dry eyes occur when your eyes do not produce enough tears or when tears evaporate too quickly. Symptoms may include burning, stinging, redness, watery eyes and blurred vision.

Persistent dry eyes should be professionally assessed to improve comfort and protect the surface of your eyes.

Blurred vision can affect one or both eyes and may develop gradually or suddenly. It may indicate a change in prescription or another eye condition requiring attention.

A comprehensive eye examination can determine the cause and recommend the most appropriate treatment.

Frequent headaches can sometimes be related to eye strain or uncorrected vision. They often occur after reading, computer work or extended periods of concentration.

An eye examination can determine whether your vision is contributing to your headaches.

Extended use of computers, tablets and smartphones can lead to tired eyes, dryness, headaches, blurred vision and neck discomfort.

Simple lifestyle changes together with the correct lenses can greatly improve comfort.

Sensitivity to light, also known as photophobia, can make bright indoor or outdoor light uncomfortable. It may be associated with dry eyes, migraines or certain eye conditions.

If light sensitivity is persistent or severe, we recommend scheduling an eye examination.

Double vision is never considered normal. It may affect one or both eyes and can indicate an underlying eye muscle or neurological condition.

If you suddenly experience double vision, seek professional medical attention as soon as possible.

Occasional floaters are common, but a sudden increase in floaters, flashes of light or a curtain over your vision should be assessed immediately.

Prompt examination can help rule out serious retinal conditions.

Persistent pain, redness or swelling may be caused by infection, inflammation or injury. These symptoms should not be ignored.

Early diagnosis and treatment help protect your vision and relieve discomfort.

Concerned About Your Vision?

If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, our experienced team is here to help. Early detection and treatment can make a significant difference to your eye health.
